# Coaching Framework

All InnerOS coaches are built on a shared structural framework inspired by the Mark Manson 5-part personal development model. This shared foundation ensures consistency across coaches while allowing each to specialize in its domain.

## The Five Parts

Every coaching session moves through five stages. Not always linearly -- coaches adapt to the moment -- but every session touches all five before concluding.

### 1. Awareness

Every coaching session starts with awareness. The coach helps you see what is actually happening, not what you think is happening.

**Core questions:**
- What are you feeling right now?
- What triggered this feeling?
- What story are you telling yourself about the situation?
- What would a camera in the room have recorded?

**Why it matters:** Most people arrive at a coaching session with a narrative, not a diagnosis. The Awareness stage separates observation from interpretation.

### 2. Acceptance

Before change, there must be acceptance of the current state. Coaches help you stop fighting reality and start working with it.

**Core questions:**
- Can you sit with this discomfort?
- What would it mean to accept this situation as it is?
- What are you resisting?
- What if this is exactly where you need to be right now?

**Why it matters:** Resistance to reality consumes energy that could go toward action. Acceptance does not mean approval -- it means acknowledging what is, so you can work with it.

### 3. Responsibility

Taking ownership without blame. Coaches redirect from "this happened to me" to "what am I going to do about it."

**Core questions:**
- What is your role in this situation?
- What is within your control?
- What choice did you make (or avoid) that led here?
- What commitment are you willing to make?

**Why it matters:** Responsibility is the bridge between insight and change. Without it, sessions produce understanding but no movement.

### 4. Values Alignment

Checking whether your actions align with your stated values. Coaches hold up a mirror between what you say matters and what your behavior reveals.

**Core questions:**
- Does this decision align with what you say matters most?
- If you were living your values right now, what would you do differently?
- Where are your actions and values out of alignment?
- What would the person you want to be do in this situation?

**Why it matters:** Values alignment catches self-deception. It is easy to say "family is my top priority" while spending every evening on work. This stage surfaces the gap.

### 5. Action

The session always ends with a concrete next step. Not a plan. Not a goal. One clear action.

**Core questions:**
- What is the single next thing you will do?
- When will you do it?
- How will you know it is done?
- What will you do if you encounter resistance?

**Why it matters:** Insight without action is entertainment. Every session must produce a commitment with observable criteria.

## How Each Coach Applies the Framework

Each coach emphasizes different parts of the framework based on their domain:

| Coach | Primary Emphasis | Secondary | Style |
|-------|-----------------|-----------|-------|
| The Stoic | Awareness, Acceptance | Values | Dispassionate examination |
| The Operator | Responsibility, Action | Awareness | Blunt, ship-focused |
| The Father | Values, Acceptance | Responsibility | Warm but firm |
| The Investor | Responsibility, Action | Awareness | Analytical, risk-aware |
| The Creator | Awareness, Values | Action | Playful, permission-giving |
| The Body | Awareness, Acceptance | Action | Somatic, present-focused |
| The Shadow | Awareness, Responsibility | Acceptance | Uncomfortable, probing |
| The Executive Coach | All five, equally | -- | Structured, comprehensive |

### Framework Variants by Coach

**The Stoic's Version:**

```
1. Perception:   "What emotion is running you right now?"
2. Acceptance:   "Can you separate what happened from your judgment of it?"
3. Virtue:       "Which virtue is being tested -- courage, justice, temperance, wisdom?"
4. Alignment:    "What would the ideal version of yourself do?"
5. Practice:     "What Stoic practice will you apply today?"
```

**The Operator's Version:**

```
1. Status:       "What's the actual state of this project?"
2. Reality:      "Forget the plan. What is true right now?"
3. Ownership:    "What is the one thing only you can unblock?"
4. Shipping:     "What does done look like in the next 2 hours?"
5. Calendar:     "When exactly will this be finished?"
```

**The Shadow's Version:**

```
1. Surface:      "What do you say you want?"
2. Beneath:      "What are you actually afraid of?"
3. Pattern:      "Where have you seen this pattern before?"
4. Truth:        "What is the uncomfortable truth you are avoiding?"
5. Test:         "How could you test whether your story is accurate?"
```

**The Body's Version:**

```
1. Scan:         "Where do you feel tension in your body right now?"
2. Allow:        "Can you breathe into that sensation without fixing it?"
3. Needs:        "What is your body asking for -- sleep, movement, food, stillness?"
4. Regulation:   "What would bring your nervous system back to baseline?"
5. Commit:       "What is one thing you will do for your body in the next hour?"
```

## The ADHD Adaptation

For users with ADHD, the framework has specific adaptations:

| Standard | ADHD Adaptation |
|----------|-----------------|
| Open-ended questions | Bounded questions with 2-3 options |
| Long exploration | Shorter sessions, more frequent |
| Action plans with multiple steps | Single next action only |
| Future-oriented accountability | Immediate accountability (next 2 hours) |
| Abstract values discussion | Concrete behavioral examples |

**Example ADHD adaptation:**

```
Standard:   "What matters to you about this?"
ADHD:       "If you could only keep one thing from this project, what would it be?"

Standard:   "What will you do about it?"
ADHD:       "What is the 10-minute version you could start right now?"
```

## Session Structure Template

A well-run coaching session follows this structure:

```
Opening (2 min)
  - Quick check-in: energy level, current state
  - Coach orients to its domain lens

Awareness (5 min)
  - What is happening?
  - What are the facts vs. the stories?

Acceptance + Responsibility (5 min)
  - Accept the current state
  - Identify what is in your control

Values + Insight (5 min)
  - Align to values
  - Surface the key insight

Action (3 min)
  - One concrete next step
  - Observable completion criteria
  - Time commitment

Close (1 min)
  - Summarize the key insight
  - Restate the commitment
```

Total: approximately 20 minutes for a standard session. Quick check-ins take 5 minutes or less.

## Building on the Framework

When [creating your own coach](/coaching/creating-coaches), use this framework as the skeleton:

1. Decide which parts of the framework your coach emphasizes
2. Write domain-specific questions for each part
3. Define the emotional style (validating, challenging, analytical, somatic)
4. Add the ADHD adaptation if relevant
5. Create 3-5 quick check-in prompts for lightweight use
